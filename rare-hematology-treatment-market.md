<p><p>The Rare Hematology Treatment Market comprises two primary types: plasma-derived factors and recombinant factors. Plasma-derived factors are obtained from human plasma and are used to treat bleeding disorders by providing essential clotting proteins. In contrast, recombinant factors are synthetically produced through genetic engineering, offering a more controlled and consistent product. Both types play crucial roles in managing conditions such as hemophilia, catering to the needs of patients while ensuring safety and efficacy in treatment options.</p></p>
